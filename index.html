The ultimate transfer argumentation system!

This website displays our project for the course Arguing Agents.
Our project tries to mimic a transfermarket for soccer teams.

How does it work?

Project

This project basically consists of two parts: argumentation within a team and argumentation between teams.

Argumentation within a team is done using a genetic algorithm, which evaluates the knowledge of five key persons of a team, the president, the captain, the trainer, the CEO and the supporters. These participants share their knowledge, each with a different degree of plausability. The goal is to come to an agreement about which player to buy.

Argumentation between teams is done using machine learning and reinforcement learning. A team evaluates its own team and available players. The different teams then wield different strategies. Top teams check whether there is a better player available than the worst player they have. If so, they bid on that player. Balanced teams ... . The goal is to come to an agreement to buy or sell a certain player for a certain amount of money.
